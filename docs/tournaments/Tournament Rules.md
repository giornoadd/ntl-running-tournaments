# Tournament Rules: Mandalorian vs IT System

Welcome to the **2026 Running Competition** between the **Mandalorian** and **IT System** teams. This document outlines the official rules, scoring mechanisms, and submission guidelines.

---

## 🏃‍♂️ Accepted Activities
The following activities are eligible for distance tracking:
- ✅ **Outdoor Run** 
- ✅ **Treadmill Run** 
- ✅ **Walk** 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Minimum Distance Requirements:**
> - **Running:** Minimum `1.0 km` per session.
> - **Walking:** Minimum `2.0 km` per session.

**Strict Prohibitions:**
- ❌ **Duplicate submissions:** One activity can only be submitted once.
- ❌ **Short distances:** Micro-activities (e.g., walking 200m to a convenience store) are disqualified.
- ❌ **Data tampering:** Modifying distance/time after the fact is strictly prohibited. Suspicious GPS data or unnatural speeds will be disqualified. Cheating results in team disqualification.

---

## ⏱️ Submission Guidelines
All results must be submitted via the internal **LINE** group.

**Required Evidence in Screenshot:**
1. Distance
2. Time/Duration
3. GPS Route Map (For outdoor activities)

> [!WARNING]
> Submit your evidence **within 24 hours** of completing the activity.

---

## 🏆 Scoring & Competition Types

The tournament is evaluated quarterly (Q1-Q4).

### 1. Team Competition (Average Distance)
The primary competition is between the two teams.
- **Metric:** Team Average Distance 
- **Formula:** `Total Team Distance` ÷ `Number of Team Members`
- *Note:* Every team member matters. Members who do not run decrease the team's overall average.

**Example:**
- **Mandalorian** (10 members) runs 100 km total = **10.0 km/person**
- **IT System** (10 members) runs 96 km total = **9.6 km/person**
- *Winner:* Mandalorian 🎉

### 2. Individual Competition (Total Distance)
A secondary competition tracks the top individual performers across both teams.
- **Metric:** Highest Total Accumulated Distance.
- **Recognition:** Top 5 participants of the quarter.
